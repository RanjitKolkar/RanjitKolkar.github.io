National Forensic Sciences University, Goa Campus

1 Laxmitara (Simarouba glauca)

A flowering tree native to Florida, South America, and the Caribbean, known as paradise-tree or bitterwood. It grows 40-50 ft tall, bears yellow flowers and purple fruits, and is used in traditional medicine, though anticancer claims lack systematic evidence.

2 Peepal Tree (Ficus religiosa)

A sacred fig native to the Indian subcontinent, revered in religious traditions. It grows up to 100 ft, supports biodiversity with heart-shaped leaves, and its leaf extracts are used in green synthesis of graphene.

Learn more

3 Guava (Psidium guajava)

A tropical fruit tree native to Central and South America, known for its vitamin-rich, edible fruits. Cultivated in warm climates, its fruits are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Learn more

4 Black Pepper (Piper nigrum)

A flowering vine from South India, cultivated for its spicy peppercorns. A key global spice, it’s used in cuisine and traditional medicine, thriving in tropical climates.

Learn more

5 Lemongrass (Cymbopogon)

A tropical grass with a citrusy flavor, used in teas, cuisine, and essential oils. It has anti-inflammatory properties and grows in warm, sunny climates.

Learn more

6 Hibiscus (Hibiscus rosa-sinensis)

A flowering plant with vibrant blooms, rich in antioxidants like anthocyanins. Used in teas and traditional medicine, it’s cultivated globally for its anti-inflammatory properties.

7 Melati China (Jasminum sambac)

A fragrant jasmine from South Asia, used in perfumes, garlands, and teas. Its white flowers are culturally significant and thrive in warm climates.

8 Badam (Prunus dulcis)

A tree from the Middle East, grown for its nutrient-rich nuts. Almonds are used in food, oils, and cosmetics, thriving in temperate climates.

9 Mango (Mangifera indica)

A tropical fruit tree from South Asia, known for its sweet, juicy fruits. Widely cultivated, its leaf extracts are used in green synthesis of graphene.

10 Custard Apple (Annona squamosa)

A small tree from the Americas, producing sweet, creamy fruits. Cultivated in tropical regions, it’s used in desserts and traditional medicine.

11 Kokum (Garcinia indica)

A tropical evergreen tree from India, known for its tangy, purple fruits. Used in culinary dishes and Ayurvedic medicine, it thrives in humid climates.

Learn more

12 Bel (Aegle marmelos)

A sacred tree from India, known as Bengal quince. Its fruits and leaves are used in Ayurvedic medicine and religious rituals, thriving in subtropical climates.

13 Amla (Phyllanthus emblica)

An Indian gooseberry tree, rich in vitamin C. Its fruits are used in Ayurvedic medicine and culinary dishes, growing in tropical and subtropical regions.

14 Neem (Azadirachta indica)

A versatile tree from India, known for its medicinal properties. Used in Ayurveda, cosmetics, and pest control, it thrives in tropical climates.
